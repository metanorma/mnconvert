<title-wrap>

Title Wrapper

Wrapper element for the title of the document.

Remarks

Usage. May occur multiple times when the title is given in multiple languages. The Language attribute is used to indicate the language of the title. For monolingual documents, the title in the principal language of the document comes first and then the title in another official language; the principal title and alternate title are styled differently. For multilingual documents, they are styled similarly.

This element is part of the ISOSTS customization of the JATS journal publishing vocabulary; it is not part of the base vocabulary.

Attributes

id Identifier
xml:lang Language

Related Elements

ISO titles may have from one to three parts. The main title (<main>) is required; it may optionally be preceded by an introductory title (<intro>) which provides context if the main title is otherwise unclear, and it may optionally be followed by a complementary title (<compl>) which provides details of the topic (and the title of the part, for multi-part standards). Model Description

The following, in order:

  • <intro> Introductory Title Element, zero or one
  • <main> Main Title Element
  • <compl> Complementary Title Element, zero or one
  • <full> Full Title

This element may be contained in:

<iso-meta>, <nat-meta>, <reg-meta>
